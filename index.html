Welcome to LUNAR

The Livermore Unit, National Association of Rocketry (LUNAR) is Section #534 of the National Association of Rocketry (NAR). We are about 300 model rocketry enthusiasts of all ages who gather together to learn rocketry, teach rocketry, exchange modeling techniques, and , of course, fly model and high-power rockets.

Flying with LUNAR

1. If you are a member, sign in at the registration table, otherwise pay $50 for a 1 year family membership, or pay a $20 walk on fee to fly for one day.

2. Get your rocket ready to fly.

3. Get a low/mid or high-power flight card and fill it out.

4. Take the rocket and card to the range safety officer's (RSO) table.

5. The safety check officer will check your rocket and assign a pad.

6. When your pad is open, take your rocket out and set it up on the pad. If you need a different rod or rail, ask, we have several different sizes.

7. When the Launch Control Officer (LCO) calls out your pad, they or you can push the button and launch it.

8. If your rocket lands in the active area of the range, wait until the LCO announces "the range is open for loading and recovery."

9. Find your rocket, bring it back and fly it again.
